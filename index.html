What is a QR Code and How Does it Work?

5 minute read  ·  Beginner guide

QR code stands for Quick Response code. It's a type of 2D barcode that can store information — like a website URL, a phone number, a WiFi password, or plain text — and can be read instantly by any smartphone camera.

How does a QR code work?

A QR code is made up of black and white squares arranged in a grid. When you point your phone camera at one, the camera reads the pattern of squares and decodes the information stored inside — all in under a second.

Unlike old barcodes that could only store about 20 numbers, a QR code can store up to 4,000 characters of text, making it far more versatile.

What can a QR code contain?

  • A website URL (most common use)
  • A WiFi network name and password
  • A phone number or email address
  • A plain text message
  • A contact card (vCard)
  • A location on a map
  • A payment link

How do I scan a QR code?

On most modern smartphones (iPhone and Android), you just open your camera app and point it at the QR code. A link or notification appears at the top of your screen — tap it to open the content.

No special app is needed on most phones made after 2018.

How to Create a QR Code for Your Business Card

4 minute read  ·  Business

Adding a QR code to your business card is one of the smartest upgrades you can make. Instead of hoping someone manually types your website URL or saves your number, they just scan the code and everything opens instantly.

What should your business card QR code link to?

  • Your website — most common, works for any business
  • Your LinkedIn profile — great for professionals and job seekers
  • Your WhatsApp — lets people message you instantly
  • A digital contact card — saves your full contact details to their phone
  • Your portfolio — ideal for designers, photographers, freelancers

How to make it in 3 steps

Step 1: Go to the QR code generator at the top of this page.

Step 2: Choose "URL", paste your website or LinkedIn link, and click Generate.

Step 3: Download the PNG and send it to your printer or add it to your business card design in Canva.

Tips for printing QR codes

  • Make the QR code at least 2cm x 2cm on the printed card — smaller than that and some phones struggle to scan it
  • Keep enough white space (quiet zone) around the edges of the code
  • Test it by scanning before you print 500 copies
  • Dark code on white background scans more reliably than light on dark

How to Create a QR Code for WiFi Sharing

3 minute read  ·  WiFi

Sharing your WiFi password by telling people to type it in is frustrating — especially with long, complex passwords. A WiFi QR code lets anyone connect instantly just by scanning it with their camera.

How to create a WiFi QR code

Step 1: Go to the QR code generator at the top of this page and click the WiFi tab.

Step 2: Enter your network name (SSID) exactly as it appears in your WiFi settings — including capitals and spaces.

Step 3: Enter your WiFi password. Choose WPA/WPA2 as the security type (this is what most modern routers use).

Step 4: Click Generate and download the PNG.

Where to display your WiFi QR code

  • Print and frame it in your living room or reception area
  • Stick it on your router
  • Add it to a welcome card for Airbnb guests
  • Put it on a menu card in a café or restaurant

Is it safe?

The QR code only works for people physically present near your router — the same people you'd give the password to anyway. The password is encoded in the QR code itself and not stored on any server when you use this generator.

QR Code vs Barcode — What's the Difference?

4 minute read  ·  Comparison

Both barcodes and QR codes store information that machines can read. But they work differently and are used in different situations.

The main difference

A traditional barcode is one-dimensional — it stores data in horizontal lines that are read left to right. It can hold up to about 20-25 numbers.

A QR code is two-dimensional — it stores data in both horizontal and vertical patterns. It can hold up to 4,000 characters including text, URLs, and more.

Quick comparison

  • Storage: Barcode = ~25 numbers. QR code = up to 4,000 characters
  • Scanning direction: Barcode = one direction only. QR code = any direction
  • What it can store: Barcode = numbers. QR code = URLs, text, WiFi, email, phone
  • Scanner needed: Barcode = special scanner. QR code = any smartphone camera
  • Damage resistance: QR codes can still be read even if up to 30% is damaged

When to use each

Use a barcode for product inventory, retail pricing, and supply chain management where dedicated scanners are already in use.

Use a QR code for marketing, customer-facing content, WiFi sharing, business cards, menus, event tickets, and anywhere a smartphone camera will be doing the scanning.

5 Creative Ways to Use QR Codes in 2026

5 minute read  ·  Tips

Most people use QR codes for one thing: linking to a website. But there are much smarter ways to use them — especially for small businesses and content creators.

1. WiFi sharing in your home or business

Print your WiFi QR code and frame it near your entrance. Guests scan it instead of asking for the password. Takes 2 minutes to set up and saves you the same conversation every time someone visits.

2. Link your social media from printed materials

Put a QR code on flyers, posters, or packaging that links directly to your Instagram, TikTok, or YouTube channel. Turn offline attention into online followers.

3. Restaurant or café menu

Link a QR code to your online menu so customers can always see your latest items and prices without reprinting menus every time something changes.

4. Event check-in

Give each event attendee a unique QR code that links to their registration details. Organisers scan it at the door for instant check-in — no paper lists needed.

5. Product packaging — link to tutorials or reviews

Add a QR code to your product packaging that links to a "how to use" video, customer reviews, or a discount for the next purchase. Turns a one-time buyer into a repeat customer.

Do Free QR Codes Expire? Everything You Need to Know

4 minute read  ·  FAQ

This is one of the most common questions people ask before creating a QR code — and the answer depends on which type of QR code you create.

Static QR codes (what this generator creates)

Static QR codes never expire. The information is encoded directly into the QR code image itself. As long as the image exists and whatever it points to (like your website) still works, the QR code will scan correctly forever.

This is what our free generator creates — static QR codes with no expiry date, no account needed, and no monthly fee.

Dynamic QR codes (paid services)

Some paid QR code services offer "dynamic" codes where you can change the destination URL after printing. These codes route through the company's servers — which means if you stop paying or the company shuts down, the code stops working.

This is why many people prefer static QR codes for anything permanent like business cards, signage, or product packaging.

What actually makes a QR code "stop working"

  • The website it points to goes offline or changes URL
  • The image is too small or damaged to scan
  • A dynamic code's service subscription expires

Bottom line

If you generate a QR code on this site pointing to a URL that stays live, that QR code will work for as long as the internet exists. No expiry, no account needed, no cost.
